# The Line Before — 3D/Interactive Version

## What this is

An interactive poetry experience for museum/gallery exhibition. Visitors read excerpts from Kahran Singh's poetry, make choices along emotional axes, and watch their chosen fragments assemble into a crossword-like structure — a new poem built from the debris of old ones.

**Live**: https://kh9010.github.io/the-line-before-3d/
**Core concept**: "a poem is always being made from other poems"

## Architecture

Hybrid DOM + canvas approach:
- **DOM** handles text (SuperpositionText morph, extraction animations, controls, title/reveal)
- **2D crossword** (DOM-positioned spans) renders the growing bananagram structure as fragments are chosen
- Data layer: 230 scored poem fragments matched to source poems in Kahran's iA Writer archive

## Key files

- `src/App.jsx` — state machine: idle → reading → extracting → complete → kept
- `src/SuperpositionText.jsx` — char-level morph between two poem-context windows (the core visual). Slow-breath pattern: hold poem A 2.2s, morph wave to B over 1s, hold B, morph back. Fog-fade makes text near the fragment legible, edges dissolve.
- `src/Crossword2D.jsx` — renders crossword placements as absolutely-positioned character spans
- `src/crosswordLayout.js` — `placeFragment()` adds one fragment incrementally to the crossword, finding intersections at shared letters. Deterministic (most intersections first, then most compact).
- `src/pairs.js` — pre-selects 4 pole-pairs per session (one per axis), ensuring different source poems per pair
- `src/phrases.js` — 230 fragments scored on 4 axes: sublimate/crystallise, grip/release, echo/ignite, smother/howl
- `src/contexts.js` — auto-generated by `build_contexts.py` in the divya repo. Maps each fragment to its source poem's context window (4 lines) with char-level fragment ranges.
- `src/rhythm.js` — Pinsky-inspired stanza break detection (rhythmic distance between consecutive fragments) and caesura line-breaking

## The 4 axes

Each fragment is scored [-1, +1] on 4 emotional axes:
- **sublimate ↔ crystallise** (dissolving ↔ sharpening)
- **grip ↔ release** (holding ↔ letting go)
- **echo ↔ ignite** (memory ↔ presence)
- **smother ↔ howl** (suppression ↔ expression)

Per session, axes are shuffled. Each round presents the two poles of one axis as buttons. The visitor's choice weights which fragment gets pulled from the pool.

## User flow

1. **Idle** — title "the line before" + tagline + begin button
2. **Reading** — two source poems morph between each other char-by-char in the morph zone. Text around each poem's fragment is legible; edges fog away. Two axis-word buttons below.
3. **Choice** — visitor picks a pole. Morph settles to the chosen poem. Non-fragment text dissolves; fragment stays and glows.
4. **Extraction** — fragment is added to the crossword above (letters cascade in). Round advances.
5. **Complete** — after 4 rounds, crossword holds still. The 4 fragment texts fade in as readable lines below. "keep" button.
6. **Kept** — crossword fades, returns to idle.

## Known issues / next steps

- **2 orphan fragments** (`spirits unmoored from flesh`, `drifting in darkness`) have no source poem in the iA Writer corpus. They're skipped during pair selection but should be replaced or sourced.
- **Excluded pseudo-sources**: `10 sample runs.md` and `Science Gallery - INTELLIGENCE Submission Draft.md` are excluded from matching (they contain fragment text in non-poem context).
- **Axis button labels** are internal scoring terms (grip, howl, etc). For museum visitors, consider renaming to more intuitive pairs or replacing with visual cues.
- **Fragment-to-crossword connection** is still a jump — ideally the extracted text would visually travel from the morph zone to the crossword.
- **Crossword can get tall** when vertical fragments are long. Auto-scaling helps but very tall layouts still feel awkward.
- **The divya repo's kahran branch** has more mature animations (flower petals, garden, ambient lights, particles) that could be brought back once the crossword direction is confirmed.

## Style

Playful, gentle, magical — not corporate. Monospace typography (Courier). Warm paper background (#f5f0e8). Minimal UI. Museum-paced (visitors may walk away — keep transitions brief, no long waits).
